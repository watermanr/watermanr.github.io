Outreach & Service

     Broadening participation in science starts with engaging  K-12 students. To this end, I did a 1.5-year Science Education and Outreach fellowship, which gave me the opportunity to help organize teacher professional development workshops, give guest lectures in high school classrooms, and lead elementary school field trips. I am also pursuing a Graduate Certificate in Community Engagement to become well-versed in best practices for this kind of work. 
   I have also worked to improve the Kellogg Biological Station community through my efforts on the Culture and Inclusion Committee. In particular, I led efforts to improve equitable access to equipment and restrooms.

Mentoring

I am dedicated to being a great mentor for a diverse range of students. In pursuit of this goal I have been the primary mentor for 11 mentees in the following groups:

  • 5 NSF Research Experiences for Undergraduate students
  • 2 KBS Undergraduate Research Assistants
  • 4 Kalamazoo Area Math & Science Center high school students

I have also been a co-mentor for 2 NSF Research Experiences for Teachers in the Conner Lab and  4 undergraduates in the Sultan Lab. 

Teaching

I have been a teaching assistant for the following courses:

  • Ecology Lab, Professor Louise Mead, Michigan State University, 2021
  • Evolution in Human-Altered Environments, Professor Sonia Sultan​, Wesleyan University, 2020

I also helped write the curriculum, organize, and teach the 8-week summer workshop series Introduction to Data Analysis in R during Summer 2024 and 2025
